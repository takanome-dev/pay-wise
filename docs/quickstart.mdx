This section will walk you through the essential steps to get up and running with "Pay Wise" in no time.

## Sign Up

Before you can start using **"Pay Wise"**, you'll need to sign up for an account. If you haven't already done so, head to our [Sign Up page](http://localhost:8080/register) to create your account.

<Info>
  The sign-up with credentials is currently disabled. We encourage you to use
  your Github account to sign up. If you really want to use email and password,
  please open an issue on our [Github
  Repo](https://github.com/takanome-dev/pay-wise/issues).
</Info>

Once you've completed the sign-up process, you'll be redirected to your dashboard.
From here, you can manage your account, create cards, and view your payment history.

## Generate Your First Card

To begin accepting payments, you'll need to generate a card for your side project. Follow these steps to get started:

1. Navigate to the **"Cards"** section, which you can find in the sidebar navigation menu.

2. Click on the **"Generate Card"** button to create a virtual card specifically for your side project. Remember, these cards are not real credit cards but are designed to securely handle payments for your projects.

3. You'll be prompted to provide some basic information about your side project and specify any relevant details. This information helps tailor the card to your project's needs.

4. Once you've provided the necessary information, click **"Generate Card"** to create your virtual card.

5. Congratulations! You've just generated your first card with **"Pay Wise"**. You can now use this card to accept payments from users of your side project.

## Obtain Your API Keys

To start using **"Pay Wise"** in your side project, you'll need to obtain your **API keys**. These keys are used to authenticate your requests to the **"Pay Wise" API**. You can find your API keys in the **settings** section of your dashboard.

And voila! You're now ready to start accepting payments with **"Pay Wise"**. Head over to the [API Reference](/api-reference/authentication) to learn more about how to use the **"Pay Wise" API**.
